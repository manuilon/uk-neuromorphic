Neuromorphic computing in the UK

Brain-inspired computing for energy-efficient AI — a coordinated national community of research, materials, and commercialisation.

Explore the ecosystem

The energy demands of AI are growing faster than conventional computing can sustainably support. Neuromorphic computing — hardware and algorithms modelled on the efficiency of the brain — offers a path to systems that are orders of magnitude more energy-efficient, enabling intelligent processing at the edge, in orbit, and at scale. The UK holds world-leading strengths across neuromorphic materials, devices, photonics, algorithms and systems. Three national initiatives now work together to turn that strength into a coherent ecosystem.

The ecosystem

Three initiatives, one national effort

Research & roadmapping

NeuroSYNC

UK Multidisciplinary Centre for Neuromorphic Systems and Computing (EPSRC-funded). A research-focused centre furthering the field and acting as the shop window for UK neuromorphic computing research. Leading the UK Neuromorphic Computing Roadmap and drawing in all relevant disciplines and stakeholders.

Visit site →

Materials & community

NeuMat

Neuromorphic Materials and Devices for Future AI Hardware. A network fostering and educating the community around neuromorphic computing, strengthening academic–industry ties, catalysing research and development, and ensuring continuity beyond the network's lifetime.

Visit site →

Commercialisation

NeuroWare IKC

Innovation and Knowledge Centre for Neuromorphic Computing. University-based, pushing existing and novel research towards commercialisation: developing IP and supporting spin-outs, licensing and scale-up.

Visit site →

Together, the three initiatives run joint networking and community building: a shared roadmap, a coherent sector voice, and a multidisciplinary research and education ecosystem for the UK.

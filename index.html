Neuromorphic computing in the UK

Brain-inspired computing for energy-efficient AI — a coordinated national community of research, networking, and commercialisation.

Explore the ecosystem

The energy demands of AI are growing faster than conventional computing can sustainably support. Neuromorphic computing — hardware and algorithms modelled on the efficiency of the brain — offers a path to systems that are orders of magnitude more energy-efficient, enabling intelligent processing at the edge, in orbit, and at scale. The UK holds world-leading strengths across neuromorphic materials, devices, photonics, algorithms and systems. Three national initiatives now work together to turn that strength into a coherent ecosystem.

The ecosystem

Three initiatives, one national effort

Research & roadmapping

NeuroSYNC

UK Multidisciplinary Centre for Neuromorphic Systems and Computing. A research-focused centre furthering the field and acting as the shop window for UK neuromorphic computing research. Leads the UK Neuromorphic Computing Roadmap — a near-term, living document setting out the core research and industrial foundations for the next ten years.

Visit site →

Networking & community

NeuMat

Neuromorphic Materials and Devices for Future AI Hardware. The UK's networking and community hub — running Network Meetings, Winter Schools and Summer Studentships, supporting early-career researchers, and acting as the sector's notice board to cross-link academia and industry.

Visit site →

Commercialisation

NeuroWare IKC

Innovation and Knowledge Centre for Neuromorphic Computing. University-based, pushing existing and novel research towards commercialisation: developing IP and supporting spin-outs, licensing and scale-up. Leads Neuromorphic Technologies: A Roadmap to 2050 — the UK's 25-year, policy-facing vision for global leadership in brain-inspired computing.

Visit site →

The three initiatives are complementary by design, not coincidence: each owns a distinct stage of the pipeline — research, networking, and commercialisation — sharing one roadmap and one voice for the UK sector.

All three initiatives are funded through UKRI EPSRC.
